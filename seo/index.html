Three Things That Drive Search Visibility

Get Indexed Correctly

Google can only rank pages it knows about. Your robots.txt controls what gets crawled, your XML sitemap tells Google which pages matter, and your redirect map prevents broken URLs from wasting crawl budget. If any of these are misconfigured, pages you care about won't get indexed — no matter how good the content is.

Earn Rich Results

Rich results — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, product prices, how-to steps — get significantly higher click-through rates than plain blue links. But they require valid structured data. The Schema Markup Generator creates JSON-LD that passes Google's Rich Results Test, and the SERP Preview Tool shows exactly how your listing will look.

Monitor & Fix Technical Issues

Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking signal — LCP, INP, and CLS all affect where you appear in mobile search. A site that loads in 4 seconds loses visitors and rankings. The Core Web Vitals Checker tests against Google's exact thresholds, and the Content Readability & Citability Scorer ensures your content is optimized for both traditional search and AI engines like Perplexity and ChatGPT.

SEO Tips for Small Businesses

1

Add structured data to your highest-value pages first

Don't try to add schema to every page at once. Start with your homepage, product pages, and FAQ pages — these benefit the most from rich results. According to Google's structured data documentation, pages with valid markup are eligible for enhanced search features that can increase click-through rates by 20-30%. The Schema Markup Generator outputs JSON-LD you can paste directly into your HTML.

2

Preview your titles and descriptions before publishing

Google rewrites roughly 60% of title tags it considers poorly optimized. If your title is too long, too vague, or doesn't match search intent, Google will replace it — often with something worse. The SERP Preview Tool shows the exact truncation points so you can craft titles that Google keeps as-is.

3

Fix page speed before chasing backlinks

A slow site with great backlinks still loses to a fast site in the rankings. Google uses LCP (Largest Contentful Paint), INP (Interaction to Next Paint), and CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) as Core Web Vitals ranking signals. Visitors bounce after 3 seconds of loading. The Core Web Vitals Checker tests your pages against Google's exact pass/fail thresholds and tells you what to fix.

4

Set up robots.txt and a sitemap before you launch

These two files are the foundation of how search engines interact with your site. A missing XML sitemap means Google guesses which pages matter. A misconfigured robots.txt can accidentally block your entire site from being indexed. Create both before launch — the Robots.txt Generator and XML Sitemap Generator handle the syntax so you don't have to.

5

Plan redirects before changing any URLs

Every broken URL is a lost ranking. During site redesigns and platform migrations, map every old URL to its new destination. Redirect chains (A redirects to B redirects to C) and loops waste crawl budget and dilute link equity. The Redirect Map Builder validates chains and loops automatically, then exports to .htaccess, nginx, or Vercel format.

6

Optimize for AI search engines, not just Google

AI-powered search engines like Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Google AI Overviews are sending real traffic now. They pull answers differently than traditional search — citing content with clear structure, specific data points, and authoritative sourcing. Format your content with descriptive headings, concrete numbers, and primary sources. The AI Search Snippet Previewer and Content Readability & Citability Scorer help you see and improve how AI engines interpret your pages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these SEO tools really free?
Yes — completely free with no account required. Every tool runs in your browser, so there's nothing to install and no usage limits. DreamHost built these to help website owners handle technical SEO without expensive subscriptions to tools like Ahrefs, Semrush, or Screaming Frog.
What is schema markup and why does it matter?
Schema markup is structured data you add to your HTML that tells search engines exactly what your page is about. It's what makes rich results possible — star ratings, FAQ dropdowns, product prices, and event details directly in Google. Without it, you're leaving those enhanced listings to competitors who do use it. The Schema Markup Generator creates valid JSON-LD for articles, products, FAQs, local businesses, and more — ready to paste into your site.
How do I preview my Google search result before publishing?
Use the SERP Preview Tool to see exactly how your title tag, meta description, and URL will appear in Google search results. It shows character truncation points and lets you test different variations side by side. Google rewrites about 60% of title tags it considers poorly written, so previewing first helps you craft titles that stick.
What are Core Web Vitals and do they affect rankings?
Core Web Vitals are three performance metrics Google uses as ranking signals: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) measures how fast your main content loads, INP (Interaction to Next Paint) measures how quickly your site responds to user input, and CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) measures visual stability. Sites that pass all three thresholds get a ranking boost in mobile search results. The Core Web Vitals Checker tests your pages against Google's exact pass/fail thresholds.
Do I need a robots.txt file?
Every website should have one. A robots.txt file tells search engine crawlers which pages to access and which to skip. Without it, bots crawl everything — including admin pages, staging content, and duplicate URLs that waste your crawl budget. The Robots.txt Generator and AI Crawler Control Center let you build rules visually for both traditional search bots and AI crawlers like GPTBot and ClaudeBot.
How does structured data help SEO?
Structured data helps Google understand your content programmatically, making you eligible for rich results — enhanced listings with stars, images, prices, FAQs, and more. Pages with rich results get significantly higher click-through rates than plain blue links. Google's documentation specifically recommends JSON-LD format, which is exactly what the Schema Markup Generator outputs. It works with any platform — WordPress, Shopify, Squarespace, or custom-built sites.
