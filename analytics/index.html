Marketing Analytics Tips for Small Businesses

1

Use the full ROI formula, not just ROAS

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) only measures revenue against ad cost. True ROI includes product costs, labor, and overhead. A campaign with 4x ROAS can actually lose money once you factor in fulfillment. Industry benchmarks suggest that the average paid search ROAS is roughly 2:1, but profitable campaigns typically need 4:1 or higher once all costs are included. The Marketing ROI Calculator will compute both so you see the real picture.

2

Never call an A/B test early

Checking results before reaching your required sample size is the single most common testing mistake. What looks like a 20% winner on day two can completely reverse by day seven. Evan Miller's analysis shows that "peeking" at tests inflates your false positive rate from 5% to as high as 30%. Calculate the sample size first, then wait until you reach it.

3

Benchmark conversion rates by industry, not globally

A 2% conversion rate is excellent for a luxury goods site but poor for a free tool signup page. Industry research consistently shows that the top 10% of landing pages convert above 11% — but that average varies wildly by vertical. E-commerce: 1.5–3%. SaaS free trials: 3–7%. Lead generation: 5–15%. The Conversion Rate Calculator will benchmark your numbers against the right comparison set for your industry.

4

Establish UTM naming conventions before your first campaign

The difference between "utm_source=Facebook" and "utm_source=facebook" creates two separate entries in Google Analytics. Multiply that across a year of campaigns and you have a data mess. Set rules up front: always lowercase, use underscores instead of spaces, and standardize terms like "email" vs "newsletter." The UTM Link Builder will enforce these conventions automatically.

5

Allocate budget based on data, not momentum

It's tempting to keep spending on the channel you started with, but businesses that reallocate budget quarterly based on performance data consistently outperform those that set-and-forget. The discipline of reviewing ROI by channel every 90 days and shifting dollars toward what's working is one of the highest-leverage habits in marketing. The Ad Spend Budget Planner will help you model different allocation scenarios and compare projected outcomes before you commit real dollars.

6

Quantify page speed in dollars, not milliseconds

Telling your team "our LCP is 3.8 seconds" gets blank stares. Telling them "slow load times are costing us $4,200 per month in lost conversions" gets action. Deloitte's Milliseconds Make Millions study found that a 0.1-second improvement in mobile site speed increased conversion rates by 8.4% for retail and 10.1% for travel. The Website Speed Impact Calculator will translate your page speed into revenue impact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these analytics tools free?
Yes — completely free with no account required. Every calculator will run in your browser with no data sent to any server. DreamHost is building these to help marketers make better decisions without expensive analytics platforms.
How do I calculate marketing ROI?
Marketing ROI = (Revenue from Marketing - Marketing Cost) / Marketing Cost x 100. For example, if you spent $1,000 on ads and generated $4,000 in revenue, your ROI is 300%. The Marketing ROI Calculator will also compute ROAS (Return on Ad Spend), CPA (Cost Per Acquisition), and break-even point so you see the full picture.
What sample size do I need for A/B testing?
It depends on three factors: your current conversion rate, the minimum improvement you want to detect, and your desired statistical confidence level (usually 95%). A page converting at 3% needs roughly 3,800 visitors per variation to detect a 1 percentage point lift. The A/B Test Sample Size Calculator will compute this for you, so you know exactly how long to run your test before drawing conclusions.
What's a good conversion rate?
The global average website conversion rate is around 2–3%, but the top 25% of sites convert at 5% or higher. What counts as "good" varies by industry: e-commerce averages 1.5–3%, SaaS free trials 3–7%, and lead generation landing pages 5–15%. The Conversion Rate Calculator will benchmark your rate against these industry averages.
What are UTM parameters?
UTM parameters are tags you add to URLs so Google Analytics (or any analytics tool) can tell you exactly where your traffic came from. There are five: utm_source (e.g., "newsletter"), utm_medium (e.g., "email"), utm_campaign (e.g., "spring_sale"), utm_term (for paid keywords), and utm_content (for A/B testing ad creatives). The UTM Link Builder will create properly formatted URLs and enforce consistent naming conventions so your data stays clean.
How does site speed affect conversions?
Significantly. According to Deloitte's Milliseconds Make Millions study, a 0.1-second improvement in mobile load time can increase conversion rates by up to 8%. Research consistently shows that pages loading in 1 second convert at roughly 3x the rate of pages loading in 5 seconds. The Website Speed Impact Calculator will estimate how much revenue you're leaving on the table based on your current load time, traffic, and conversion rate.
