Who These Tools Are For

Legal compliance shouldn't require a retainer. These tools handle the policies and checks that every website needs.

Small Business Owners

You launched your site, added a contact form, installed Google Analytics, and now you're technically collecting personal data without the legal pages to back it up. A privacy policy and cookie banner aren't optional anymore — GDPR and CCPA have real fines. These tools generate compliant pages in minutes so you can focus on running your business, not deciphering regulations.

E-commerce Store Owners

You process payments, collect shipping addresses, set cookies for cart persistence, and probably retarget visitors with ads. That's at least four privacy regulations intersecting at once. Beyond your privacy policy, you need a return policy that matches your actual practices, terms of service that protect your intellectual property, and cookie consent that covers your tracking pixels. One missing page can mean a chargeback dispute you lose by default.

SaaS Founders

Your app handles user data, processes subscriptions, and probably integrates with third-party services. That means you need terms of service with acceptable use policies, a privacy policy that covers data processing agreements, and GDPR compliance if even one EU user signs up. Enterprise clients will ask for these documents before they even start a trial. Having them ready signals that your product is mature enough to trust with their data.

Freelancers & Consultants

Your portfolio site has a contact form. Your blog uses analytics. Maybe you embed a Calendly widget. Each of those touches personal data. You also need disclaimers if you give professional advice — financial, medical, legal, or otherwise. These generators handle the boilerplate so you can get protected in an afternoon, not a billable hour.

Marketing Agencies

You build websites for clients, which means you're setting up the legal infrastructure too. Instead of copying a competitor's privacy policy (which exposes your client to liability), generate one that actually matches their data practices. Run an accessibility check before handoff so the client doesn't get hit with an ADA demand letter six months later. These tools make compliance a standard part of your delivery process.

Legal & Compliance Tips for Website Owners

1

Your privacy policy must match your actual data practices

A generic privacy policy that mentions data you don't collect — or omits data you do collect — is worse than having none at all. If you use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, Hotjar, or any third-party tool that tracks visitors, each one must be disclosed. According to GDPR Enforcement Tracker, inaccurate privacy policies are among the most-fined violations in the EU.

2

Cookie consent requires real choices, not just a notice

A banner that says "We use cookies" with only an "OK" button does not meet GDPR requirements. The European Data Protection Board requires that users can accept or reject non-essential cookies before they're set. That means analytics and marketing cookies must be off by default until the visitor actively opts in.

3

Accessibility failures are the fastest-growing source of website lawsuits

According to ADA Title III research, web accessibility lawsuits have increased year over year since 2017. The most common issues are missing alt text on images, insufficient color contrast, and forms without proper labels. WCAG 2.2 Level AA is the standard courts reference most often — and the Website Accessibility Checker audits against exactly those criteria.

4

GDPR applies to your website if anyone from the EU visits it

This is the part most U.S. business owners miss. GDPR is not about where your business is located — it's about where your visitors are. If a single person in France or Germany visits your site and you collect any data (even via Google Analytics), GDPR applies. CCPA is similar for California residents. The GDPR/CCPA Compliance Checker tells you exactly which regulations affect you based on your traffic.

5

Terms of service protect you from disputes you haven't imagined yet

Without terms of service, you have no agreed-upon rules for user behavior, no limitation of liability, and no defined jurisdiction for disputes. If a user posts harmful content on your site, copies your work, or files a chargeback, your terms of service are your first line of defense. According to Nolo's legal guidance, enforceable ToS require clear acceptance mechanisms — not just a footer link.

6

Return policies must be visible before purchase, not just after

The FTC requires that return and refund terms are disclosed before a transaction. Hiding them in a footer link isn't enough if customers don't see them during checkout. A clear, accessible return policy also reduces chargebacks — Visa and Mastercard both factor return policy visibility into dispute rulings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these legal tools free?
Yes — completely free with no account required. Every tool runs in your browser, so there's nothing to install and no usage limits. DreamHost built these to help website owners handle basic legal compliance without paying a lawyer for boilerplate pages. That said, these tools generate standard documents — if you have complex legal needs or operate in a heavily regulated industry, you should have an attorney review the output.
Do I need a privacy policy?
If your website collects any personal data — including email addresses, cookies, analytics, or contact form submissions — you are legally required to have a privacy policy in most jurisdictions. GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), PIPEDA (Canada), and many other laws mandate it. Even if you only use Google Analytics, you need one. The Privacy Policy Generator creates a customized policy based on what data you actually collect and which regulations apply to you.
What is GDPR and does it apply to me?
GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is an EU law that protects personal data. It applies to you if you have any visitors from the EU — regardless of where your business is located. If someone in Germany visits your website and you collect their data via analytics, cookies, or forms, GDPR applies. Penalties for non-compliance can reach 4% of annual global revenue or 20 million euros, whichever is higher. The GDPR/CCPA Compliance Checker helps you determine your obligations and identify gaps.
How do I make my website accessible?
Start with WCAG 2.2 Level AA — the most widely accepted standard. Key areas include: adding alt text to all images, ensuring keyboard navigation works on every page, maintaining a color contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text, labeling all form fields, and using proper heading hierarchy (H1, H2, H3 in order). The Website Accessibility Checker walks you through each requirement with specific fix instructions, and the Color Contrast Checker tests your color combinations against WCAG thresholds.
What should my cookie banner say?
Under GDPR, your cookie banner must clearly state what cookies you use and why, give users the option to accept or reject non-essential cookies before they are set, and link to your full cookie policy. A simple "We use cookies" banner with only an "OK" button is not compliant. You need granular consent controls — analytics, marketing, and functional cookies should each be separately opt-in. The Cookie Policy & Banner Generator creates both the policy document and a compliant banner with category controls.
Do I need terms of service?
Terms of service aren't legally required in most places, but they protect your business. Without them, you have no defined rules for user behavior, no limitation of liability, no intellectual property protections, and no dispute resolution process. If you sell anything, accept user-generated content, or run a SaaS product, terms of service are essential. The Terms of Service Generator creates a customized document based on your business type, payment model, and jurisdiction.
