Three Workflows That Make Copy Convert

Write Headlines That Get Clicks

Headlines determine whether anyone reads the rest. As David Ogilvy wrote in Confessions of an Advertising Man, on average five times as many people read the headline as read the body copy — meaning your headline is worth 80 cents of every dollar you spend on a piece of content. Run yours through the Headline Analyzer to score word balance, sentiment, and length. Then check readability with the Readability Optimizer to make sure the body copy holds their attention once they click.

Build Product Descriptions That Sell

Great product copy follows a structure. Nielsen Norman Group research found that the majority of observed e-commerce purchase failures were caused by poor content — incomplete descriptions, missing details, or unclear product information. Use the Product Description Builder to generate structured copy using AIDA or PAS, then add a CTA from the CTA Generator that matches the buying stage.

Inconsistent tone makes your brand feel like five different people wrote it — because they probably did. Paste your strongest content into the Tone & Voice Analyzer to create a voice profile, then use it as a checklist every time someone new writes for your brand.

Copywriting Tips for Small Businesses

1

Aim for a readability score of 60 or higher

The Flesch Reading Ease scale runs from 0 (academic) to 100 (simple). Most marketing copy should land between 60 and 70 — that's conversational enough for the general public without sounding dumbed down. Studies show that higher readability correlates with higher engagement. The Copy Readability Optimizer scores your text and highlights the specific sentences dragging your score down.

2

Use the 4 U's for headlines: Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific, Useful

"How to Save Money" is generic. "How to Cut Your Grocery Bill by 30% This Month" hits all four U's. The best headlines promise a specific benefit with a clear timeframe. Test multiple versions in the Headline Analyzer — even small word changes can shift a headline from generic to compelling.

3

Lead every CTA with a verb

"Start," "Get," "Download," "Join" — action verbs tell people exactly what happens when they click. Avoid vague labels like "Submit" or "Click Here." Specific CTA text sets clear expectations, reduces friction, and gives visitors confidence about what happens next — which is why conversion rate optimization experts consistently recommend verb-led buttons. The CTA Generator creates variations grouped by psychology principle — urgency, social proof, loss aversion — so you can test what resonates.

4

A/B test your copy before you commit

Don't guess which version is better — measure it. Write two versions of your landing page headline, product description, or email subject line, then paste both into the Before/After Copy Comparer. It shows a word-level diff alongside readability scores for each version, so you can see exactly what changed and whether it improved.

5

Nail your value proposition before writing anything else

If you can't explain why someone should choose you in one sentence, no amount of clever copy will save the page. The Value Proposition Generator forces clarity by making you fill in the blanks of five proven frameworks. The output gives you a foundation — every headline, CTA, and product description should reinforce it.

6

Use storytelling frameworks to make your brand relatable

Facts tell, stories sell. In a well-known Stanford experiment, audiences recalled stories from student presentations far more easily than they recalled statistics — only 5% could remember an individual stat, but 63% remembered the stories. Structure is what makes the difference. The Story Framework Generator turns your brand story into five narrative formats — Hero's Journey, PAS, BAB, Pixar Story Spine, and Customer Success Arc — with ready-to-post social snippets for each.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these copywriting tools free?
Yes — all nine tools are completely free with no account, no login, and no usage limits. They run entirely in your browser, so there's nothing to install. DreamHost built them so small businesses and marketers can write better copy without paying for expensive software.
What makes a good headline?
Strong headlines balance four word types: common words (for readability), uncommon words (for interest), emotional words (for engagement), and power words (for urgency). The best headlines are 6 to 12 words, front-load the benefit, and match the reader's search intent. The Headline Analyzer scores yours against these criteria and shows where the balance is off.
How do I improve the readability of my copy?
Aim for a Flesch Reading Ease score of 60 or higher — that means most adults can understand it without re-reading. Cut sentences over 20 words, replace jargon with plain language, and break long paragraphs into shorter ones. The Copy Readability Optimizer gives sentence-by-sentence feedback and highlights exactly where readers are likely to drop off.
What's a value proposition?
A value proposition is a clear statement of why someone should choose your product or service over the alternatives. It answers three questions: What do you do? Who is it for? Why is it better? The Value Proposition Generator creates five versions using proven frameworks — Steve Blank, Geoffrey Moore, USP, Elevator Pitch, and Tagline — so you can pick the one that fits your audience.
How long should a CTA be?
The most effective CTAs are 2 to 5 words. "Start Free Trial" outperforms "Click here to start your free trial today" because it's scannable and action-oriented. Lead with a verb, state the benefit, and cut everything else. The CTA Generator creates variations at different lengths so you can A/B test what works for your audience.
What is the AIDA framework?
AIDA stands for Attention, Interest, Desire, Action — a four-step structure for persuasive copy. You grab attention with a hook, build interest with relevant details, create desire by showing the benefit, and close with a clear call to action. The Product Description Builder uses AIDA alongside PAS and FAB frameworks to generate complete product descriptions you can use immediately.
