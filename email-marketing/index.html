Email Marketing Tips for Small Businesses

1

Keep subject lines between 30 and 50 characters

Most mobile email clients truncate subject lines after 35–40 characters. If your key message gets cut off, open rates drop. Industry benchmark data shows that subject lines in this range consistently outperform longer ones. Run yours through the Subject Line Tester to check length, spam triggers, and emotional impact before sending.

2

Write preheader text that complements — not repeats — your subject line

The preheader is the second line of copy subscribers see in their inbox. If you leave it blank, most clients will pull the first line of your email body — often "View in browser" or an unsubscribe link. Use the Preheader Preview to see exactly how your subject + preheader pair looks across Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, and mobile.

3

Scan every email for spam triggers before sending

Words like "free," "guarantee," and "limited time" aren't automatic death sentences, but stacking them with ALL CAPS and excessive exclamation points will get you flagged. The Deliverability Checker scans for 300+ known spam triggers, checks your image-to-text ratio, validates links, and verifies CAN-SPAM compliance — all in one pass.

4

Always include a plain text fallback

Corporate email servers, accessibility tools, and privacy-focused clients often strip or block HTML. A well-formatted plain text version ensures every subscriber can read your message. It also signals to spam filters that you're a legitimate sender. The Plain Text Email Formatter converts your HTML to clean text with preserved headings, lists, and links in seconds.

5

Use bulletproof buttons — not image-based CTAs

Image-based buttons disappear when images are blocked, which is the default in many Outlook configurations. An HTML/CSS button with a VML fallback for Outlook renders everywhere. The Email CTA Button Generator creates production-ready code with full Outlook support — just customize colors, size, and copy.

6

Benchmark your metrics against your industry, not generic averages

A 15% open rate might be terrible for a nonprofit newsletter but perfectly normal for retail promotions. Context matters more than absolute numbers. The Email Metrics Calculator benchmarks your open rate, click-through rate, CTOR, and bounce rate against 20 specific industry averages — so you know whether you have a problem or just a different audience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these email marketing tools really free?
Yes — completely free with no account required. Every tool runs in your browser, so there's nothing to install and no usage limits. DreamHost built these to help small businesses and marketers improve their email campaigns without expensive software subscriptions.
What makes a good email subject line?
A good subject line is 30–50 characters, creates curiosity or urgency without being spammy, and matches the email content. Avoid ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, and spam trigger words like "free money" or "act now." Use the Email Subject Line Tester to score your subject lines on length, power words, spam triggers, and personalization before you send.
How do I improve email deliverability?
Start by scanning your email content for spam triggers — words, phrases, and formatting patterns that spam filters flag. Check your image-to-text ratio (keep it under 40% images), include an unsubscribe link for CAN-SPAM compliance, and avoid link shorteners. The Email Deliverability Checker scans for 300+ known issues in seconds.
Why do I need a plain text version of my email?
Many email clients — especially corporate ones — block HTML by default. A plain text fallback ensures every subscriber can read your message. It also improves deliverability because spam filters are suspicious of HTML-only emails. The Plain Text Email Formatter converts your HTML email to clean, readable plain text with preserved structure.
What's a good email open rate?
The average open rate across all industries is roughly 21%, but it varies significantly — nonprofits average 28% while retail sits closer to 17%. What matters more is how you compare to your specific industry. The Email Metrics Calculator benchmarks your open rate, CTR, CTOR, and bounce rate against 20 industry averages so you know exactly where you stand.
How do I grow my email list?
Focus on three levers: increasing signup rate (better lead magnets, optimized forms), reducing churn (fewer unsubscribes through better content), and maintaining list hygiene (removing inactive addresses). The Email List Growth Calculator lets you model different scenarios — see how doubling your signup rate or halving your churn affects your list size over 12 months.
