Social Media Tips for Small Businesses

1

Get your image sizes right the first time

A landscape photo that looks great on Facebook will get cropped awkwardly on Instagram and look tiny on Pinterest. Every platform has specific dimensions: Instagram feed posts are 1080x1080px, Stories are 1080x1920px, LinkedIn images are 1200x627px. Rather than resizing manually for each one, upload once to the Social Image Resizer and export every size in one batch.

2

Use fewer, more relevant hashtags

The "30 hashtags on every Instagram post" era is over. Instagram's own creators account now recommends 3-5 highly relevant hashtags. On LinkedIn, 3-5 perform best. On X, more than 2 actually decreases engagement. The Hashtag Generator organizes suggestions by category — niche, trending, and community — so you pick the right ones, not just the most popular ones.

3

Write to each platform's character limits

X truncates at 280 characters. LinkedIn shows "...see more" after about 140 characters on mobile. Instagram captions can be 2,200 characters but only the first 125 show above the fold. Writing one caption and pasting it everywhere means it's optimized for nothing. The Platform Character Counter shows live counts against 8 platform limits with truncation previews so you can see exactly where your text gets cut off.

4

Post when your audience is actually online

A great post published at 11 PM on a Sunday will get buried. Timing varies dramatically by platform and industry — a B2B audience on LinkedIn is most active Tuesday through Thursday mornings, while Instagram engagement for retail peaks on weekends. The Best Time to Post tool shows a heatmap of optimal windows by platform and industry, auto-adjusted to your timezone.

5

Make your bio work harder than your feed

Your bio is the most-viewed piece of text on your profile, but most businesses treat it as an afterthought. Each platform has different character limits, formatting rules, and conventions. A LinkedIn bio that reads like an Instagram bio looks unprofessional. The Bio Generator creates platform-optimized versions from your business details, so each one feels native to where it lives.

6

Fix your Open Graph tags before sharing links

When you share a blog post or product page on social media, the preview card is controlled by Open Graph meta tags in your HTML. If they're missing or misconfigured, Facebook might pull a random image, LinkedIn might show a blank card, and your click-through rate drops. The Open Graph Debugger validates your tags and shows you exactly what the preview will look like on every platform before you post.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these social media tools free?
Yes — every tool is completely free with no account, no login, and no usage limits. They run entirely in your browser, so there's nothing to install. DreamHost built these to help small businesses and creators manage social media without paying for Hootsuite, Buffer, or Canva subscriptions.
What size should my social media images be?
Every platform has different requirements. Instagram feed posts are 1080x1080px, Stories are 1080x1920px, LinkedIn shared images are 1200x627px, Facebook posts are 1200x630px, and X (Twitter) posts are 1600x900px. The Social Image Resizer lets you upload one image and export it in every platform's exact dimensions automatically.
How many hashtags should I use?
It depends on the platform. Instagram allows up to 30 but 3-5 highly relevant hashtags now perform best. LinkedIn posts do well with 3-5 hashtags. X (Twitter) works best with 1-2. TikTok benefits from 3-5 trending and niche hashtags. The Hashtag Generator suggests categorized hashtags by platform so you're not guessing.
When is the best time to post on social media?
It varies by platform and industry. Generally, Tuesday through Thursday between 9 AM and 12 PM tend to see higher engagement across most platforms. But a fitness brand posting on Instagram has a completely different peak window than a B2B SaaS company posting on LinkedIn. The Best Time to Post tool shows an interactive heatmap filtered by your platform and industry, auto-adjusted to your local timezone.
How do I check my engagement rate?
Engagement rate is calculated by dividing total engagements (likes, comments, shares, saves) by your follower count or reach, then multiplying by 100. A good engagement rate varies by platform — 1-3% is solid on Instagram, while 0.5-1% is strong on Facebook. The Engagement Rate Calculator handles the math across 5 platforms and benchmarks your result against industry averages so you know where you stand.
What are Open Graph tags?
Open Graph (OG) tags are HTML meta tags that control how your page looks when shared on social media. They set the title, description, and image that appear in the link preview card on Facebook, LinkedIn, X, and Slack. Without them, platforms guess — and usually guess wrong. The Open Graph Debugger lets you validate your OG tags and preview exactly what your share cards will look like before you post.
